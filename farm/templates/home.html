Avocado Farming

Soil & Climate Requirements

Soil

Requires deep, fertile, well-drained sandy loam soil (pH 5.5-7.0). Highly sensitive to waterlogging.

Climate

Sub-tropical climate; thrives in regions with no frost and high humidity. Must be protected from strong winds.

Inputs & Equipment

  • High-quality grafted saplings (e.g., Hass, Fuerte)
  • Heavy organic manure and balanced NPK fertilizers
  • Drip irrigation system for precise watering
  • Orchard management tools (pruners, saws, sprayers)
  • Mulching materials to conserve soil moisture

Cotton Farming

Soil & Climate Requirements

Soil

Deep, well-drained black cotton soils or alluvial soils are ideal, with good moisture retention (pH 6.0-8.0).

Climate

A tropical/sub-tropical crop requiring a long frost-free period (200+ days), high temperatures, and sunny weather during maturation.

Inputs & Equipment

  • High-yielding, pest-resistant seeds (e.g., Bt cotton hybrids)
  • Balanced application of NPK fertilizers
  • Irrigation source for critical growth stages
  • Tractors, cultivators, and sprayers
  • Chemicals for pest (e.g., bollworm) and weed control

Mushroom Farming

Substrate & Climate Requirements

Substrate

Grows on organic substrates like paddy straw, wheat straw, composted manure, or sawdust, not soil.

Climate

Requires a controlled indoor environment with high humidity (80-95%), low light, and specific temperatures (varying by species).

Inputs & Equipment

  • High-quality mushroom spawn (the "seed")
  • Sterilized substrate material (e.g., straw)
  • A dedicated growing room or shed with racks
  • Sprayers or foggers to maintain humidity
